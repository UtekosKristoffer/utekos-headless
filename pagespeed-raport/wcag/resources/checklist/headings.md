# Headings

| Topic                                | Technique | WCAG AA Requirement |
| ------------------------------------ | --------- | ------------------- |
| Headings to Bypass Blocks of Content |

[Bypass Blocks:](https://dequeuniversity.com/class/semantic-structure-wcag-2.2/within-pages/)
Screen readers allow users to navigate by headings, so headings are an effective
way to bypass blocks of content, as required by WCAG 2.4.1.

**Note:** Headings are not absolutely required by WCAG to pass 2.4.1, but are
highly recommended, along with landmarks and skip links.

[Testing methodology 2.4.1.a](https://dequeuniversity.com/class/dequeway/interpreting/wcag/a/2.4.1/2.4.1.a)

| Required  
[WCAG 2.4.1](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/bypass-blocks) | |
Meaningful Text |
[Accurate, Informative Section Labels](https://dequeuniversity.com/class/semantic-structure-wcag-2.2/headings/meaningful#descriptive-text):
Headings MUST be accurate and informative, as labels for the sections of text
they describe.

[Testing methodology 1.3.1.e](https://dequeuniversity.com/class/dequeway/interpreting/wcag/a/1.3.1/1.3.1.e)

[Use Real Headings:](https://dequeuniversity.com/class/semantic-structure-wcag-2.2/headings/)

true heading (`<h1>`, `<h2>`, etc.) in the markup. | Required  
[WCAG 1.3.1](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/info-and-relationships) |

Text that does not act as a heading visually or structurally MUST NOT be marked
as a heading.

| Outline/Hierarchy of Content |

[Content Outline:](https://dequeuniversity.com/class/semantic-structure-wcag-2.2/headings/outline#content-outline)
Headings SHOULD convey a clear and accurate structural outline of the sections
of content of a web page.

| Best Practice | |
[Consecutive Levels:](https://dequeuniversity.com/class/semantic-structure-wcag-2.2/headings/outline#hierarchical-order)
